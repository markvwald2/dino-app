Come Sail Away

Ouranosaurus had a sail on its back which was formed by long-spined vertebrae in the backbone. The sail was probably used for temperature regulation in the hot, African environment, and would help the animal cool down in the heat of day by dispersing heat. It's possible it also collected heat early in the cool mornings when the sail faced the sun.

Say My Name:

ore-ANN-oh-SORE-us

Slow Your Roll

Ouro could run on two legs or walk on four, but he was typically slow and bulky.



Five-Finger Discount

Ouranosaurus had five short fingers on each hand. The second and third fingers had hoof-like nails which were good for grazing low-lying plants. Each thumb had a small conical spike like Iguanodon.
