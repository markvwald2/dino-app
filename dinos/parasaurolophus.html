Bone-headed Dino

Parasaurolophus was a long-crested, duck-billed dinosaur. Its extremely long, backwards-leaning, hollow, bony crest was bigger than the rest of its skull and may have been used to produce a low-frequency, foghorn-like sound. The Parasaurolophus' nostrils at the end of its snout went up through the crest and back down it, forming four tubes, and likely enhanced its sense of smell.

Say My Name:

pa-ra-saw-ROL-off-us

Two legs or Four?

Parasaurolophus walked and ran on two legs, but it probably went on all fours to forage for plants. It may have spent some time in the water.



Eggs in the Nest

Parasaurolophus may have nested in colonies, digging a shallow hole in soft ground and laying up to twenty eggs in each nest, covered by vegetation.
