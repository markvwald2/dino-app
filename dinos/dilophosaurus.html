Double-Crested Dino

The most distinctive feature of Dilophosaurus was the paired crests on top of its skull, the function of which remains a mystery. Most likely, these crests were either to attract mates or they helped members of packs to recognize each other.

Say My Name:

die-LOF-oh-SAWR-us

Spitting Image

Although some believe the Dilophosaurus spit poison on its pray, there is no fossil evidence that it did so.



Sticking Together

Dilophosaurus may have roved in packs, since three specimens were found together in Arizona.
