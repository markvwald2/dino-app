Abundant Camarasaurus

Pound for pound, the most common giant herbivore of late Jurassic North America was Camarasaurus. This medium-sized dinosaur, which weighed "only" about 20 tons, is believed to have roamed the midwestern plains in herds, and was probably a prime source of food for the hungry carnivores like Allosaurus.

Say My Name:

cam-AH-rah-SORE-us

Fast Grower

The most famous Camarasaurus skeleton is 3-meters long and had grown that large in only two years, having hatched from an egg no bigger then a soccer ball.



Hollow Bones

Deep pockets or chambers in the spine of Camarasaurus made its skeleton lighter. That's how it got its name, which means "chambered reptile".
