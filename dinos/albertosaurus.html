Lean, Mean Eating Machine

Albertosaurus was about half the size of T. Rex, but he was every bit as dangerous and, considering its more petite size, possibly faster and nimbler in the pursuit of prey. He made its living by hunting numerous herbivores, probably making a specialty out of slow-witted hadrosaurs, or duck-billed dinosaurs.

Say My Name:

al-BERT-oh-SORE-us

Bro of the Gorgo?

Some paleontologists insist that Gorgosaurus should properly be classified as a species of Albertosaurus, though not all experts agree.



Pack Your Lunch

Albertosaurus may have hunted in packs, in which case not even the armored, full-grown plant eaters of late Cretaceous North America such as Triceratops would have been safe from being turned into a quick lunch.
