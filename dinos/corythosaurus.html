As Loud as a Fog Horn

The most distinctive feature of Corythosaurus was the prominent crest on its head, which looked a bit like the helmet worn by the ancient Greeks of Corinth. Corythosaurus likely used its crest to signal to other dinosaurs with the extremely loud, booming sound it produced when funneled with blasts of air.

Say My Name:

core-ith-oh-SORE-us

Complete Fossil

The first Corythosaurus fossil was discovered in 1912 in Alberta, Canada. It included a complete skeleton as well as Corythosaurus' fossilized skin.



Band Member?

When Corythosaurus pushed air through the hollow crest on its skull, it likely made noises similar to that of a trumpet or tuba.
