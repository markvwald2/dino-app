Armored Like a Tank

The entire top side of Ankylosaurus was heavily protected from carnivores with thick, oval plates embedded in its leathery skin, with two rows of spikes along its body. It also had large horns that projected from the back of its head, and a club-like tail. It even had bony plates as protection for its eyes. Only its under-belly was unplated, so flipping it over was the only way to wound it.

Say My Name:

ann-key-low-SAWR-us

In Da Club

It's unclear whether Ankylosaurus swung its tail to keep predators away, or if the club was just for impressing other Ankylosaurus.



Last to Go Extinct

The armor and slow metabolism of Ankylosaurus enabled it to weather extinction better than most dinosaurs, after vast clouds of dust circled the earth in the wake of the meteor impact.
