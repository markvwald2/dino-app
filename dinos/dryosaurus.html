Teenage Dino

Dryosaurus had a long neck, long, slender legs and a long, stiff tail. Its arms, however, with five fingers on each hand, were short. Known specimens were about 8 to 14 feet long and weighed 170 to 200 pounds. However, the adult size is unknown, as no known adult specimens of the dryosaurus have been found.

Say My Name:

dry-oh-SAWR-us

Fleet of Foot

This small herbivore was both swift and agile. Lacking any other defensive abilities, its only means of defense was to flee any predators.



Leaf Teeth

Dryosaurus' name, "oak lizard," refers to the shape of some of its teeth, which scientists believe have the same shape as that of oak leaves.
