Thick Headed Dino

The top of a Pachycephalosaurus skull could be 9 inches thick, and much of the skull and the snout were also covered by small bony knobs and spikes. Some scientists believe his padded skull allowed him to head-butt other dinosaurs, much like bighorn sheep and other modern animals do.

Say My Name:

pack-ee-sef-ah-low-SORE-us

Fight or Flight?

Pachycephalosaurs were herding dinosaurs that lived in small groups in coastal and upland regions. Running was probably the first line of self-defense.



Close to the Ground

When Pachycephalosaurus ran, it probably held its back level to the ground. It may have gone on all fours to forage for low-lying plants.
