Not Named for a Bull

From its name, you might think Torosaurus was named after a bull. After all, "toro" is "bull" in Spanish. But the truth is that "toro" in this case means "perforated" or "pierced," which refers to the large holes in this herbivore's skull, beneath its enormous frill.

Say My Name:

TORE-oh-SORE-us

Cheeky Teeth

Torosaurus had well-developed teeth in its cheek that helped it pulverize the plants it stripped with its sharp beak.



Big Headed

Torosaurus was one of the largest horned dinosaurs to have ever lived, and also had the second largest skull of any land animal ever.
