The Fishing Dinosaur

Baryonyx had large front claws, a long, narrow skull and muscular arms. Paleontologists believe that Baryonyx prowled riverbanks and lakebeds, spearing passing fish with its claws much like a modern bear. In fact, the remains of prehistoric fish have been found in the stomach of one Baryonyx specimen.

Say My Name:

bah-RYE-oh-nix

Recently Discovered

The first Baryonyx fossil (mainly just a fossilized claw) wasn't discovered until 1983, by an amateur fossil hunter in England.



A New Name?

There's another dinosaur named Suchosaurus, discovered in the 1800's, which may actually have been a Baryonyx. If so, Baryonyx would be renamed Suchosaurus, just like when Brontosaurus was renamed Apatasaurus.
