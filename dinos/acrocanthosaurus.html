Spiked-back Dino

Acrocanthosaurus was a fierce predator who could kill even large dinosaurs such as Brachiosaurus and Apatosaurus. It had 17-inch long spikes growing out of its back, neck and tail that may have formed a thick, fleshy sail on its spine. It had powerful arms, and each hand had three fingers equipped with long, sickle-like claws.

Say My Name:

Hump Day

Acro's distinctive back ridge may have served as a as a temperature-control device or even a storage place for fat, similar to the hump of a modern camel.



Shy Dino

Acrocanthosaurus fossils are very, very rare. Only four skeletal specimens have been found and most of those are just small fragments or teeth.
